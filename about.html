Zane Yousefi Sohi [cite: 1]

Hydrologist · Climate Scientist · Machine Learning Researcher

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Hydrology and Water Resources at the University of Arizona[cite: 5, 6]. My research focuses on the intersection of climate science, hydrologic modeling, and artificial intelligence.

I specialize in designing and training state-of-the-art deep learning models, including U-Net architectures and Corrective Diffusion (CorrDiff) [cite: 18], to perform high-resolution statistical downscaling of climate data[cite: 18]. I build high-performance data pipelines using Python, xarray, Dask, and Zarr to process terabyte-scale geoscientific datasets (like ERA5 and AORC) on HPC clusters[cite: 19, 28].

Ultimately, my goal is to couple these downscaled AI-generated outputs with land-surface models (like Noah-MP and VIC-WUR) to improve hydrologic forecasting, water availability assessment, and drought simulation[cite: 20, 29, 40].
