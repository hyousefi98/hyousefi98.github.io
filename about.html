I am Hossein (Zane) Yousefi Sohi, a Ph.D. student in Hydrology and Water Resources at the University of Arizona, where I work under the supervision of Dr. Ali Behrangi and Dr. Andrew Bennett. My research lies at the intersection of hydrology, climate science, and machine learning—developing next-generation generative AI and deep learning frameworks to better simulate, downscale, and forecast hydroclimatic processes across scales.

With a cumulative GPA of 4.0/4.0 throughout my academic career, I am committed to building high-performance data pipelines and interpretable models that connect scientific theory with real-world hydrologic applications. My work integrates large-scale datasets such as ERA5, AORC, and IMERG with physics-informed deep learning approaches to improve predictions of precipitation, runoff, and terrestrial water storage in data-scarce regions.

Core Research Competencies

  • Generative AI for Climate Modeling: Designing and training state-of-the-art deep learning models (U-Net, Corrective Diffusion) in PyTorch for high-resolution statistical downscaling and climate forecasting.
  • High-Performance Data Pipelines: Engineering scalable workflows using xarray, Dask, and Zarr to process terabyte-scale ERA5 and AORC data efficiently on HPC clusters.
  • Hydrologic Application & Validation: Coupling downscaled climate outputs with hydrologic models (Noah-MP, VIC-WUR) and validating results using metrics such as NSE, KGE, and CRPS.
