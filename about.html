I am Hossein Yousefi Sohi, a Ph.D. student in Hydrology and Water Resources at the University of Arizona. My research integrates machine learning, large-domain hydrologic modeling, and climate data analysis to improve the understanding and prediction of precipitation, groundwater, and surface water processes—particularly across arid and semi-arid regions.

I am advised by Dr. Ali Behrangi and collaborate with researchers at NCAR and the Colorado School of Mines. My work focuses on AI-driven downscaling, bias correction of precipitation products, and stochastic diffusion modeling for rainfall and hydrologic forecasting. I am passionate about developing interpretable, data-driven tools that enhance water-resource decision-making under climate change.
