Zane Yousefi Sohi

Hydrologist · Climate Scientist · Machine Learning Researcher

Ph.D. candidate in Hydrology and Water Resources at the University of Arizona. I build AI-driven models and high-performance data pipelines to downscale, evaluate, and apply hydro-climate datasets for decision-ready water resources insights—especially in arid and semi-arid regions.

My current work combines U-Net architectures, corrective diffusion, and scalable Python stacks (xarray · Dask · Zarr · PyTorch) to produce credible, high-resolution forcings and to couple them with land-surface and hydrologic models.
